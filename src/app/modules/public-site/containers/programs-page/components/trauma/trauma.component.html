Trauma Focused Foster Care Program

A program that is trauma-focused has pre-service training for caregivers which covers topics such as, but not limited to: sexual assault and exploitation, human trafficking, severe neglect, general maltreatment. The purpose of having a trauma-informed care program and foster home environment is to have caregivers who understand the range of traumatic experiences and the emotional by-products that manifest in abnormal social interactions, self-harm, substance abuse, multiple sexual partners, interactions with law enforcement.

Caregivers trained in trauma-focused care will be more accepting, accommodating and understanding of the issues the youth placed with Safe Harbours face. They will have a better understanding of the treatment options available, their efficacy and target patient group. Trauma-informed caregivers will have more meaningful dialog with mental health service professionals if they have a firm understanding of the many components of PTSD and other trauma-related disorders.

The per diem - amount per day, per child given to the foster parent(s) - is $95 - $105 (i.e. two (2) children @ $95/day multiplied by a thirty (30) day month = $5700 - or $69,350 per year.
